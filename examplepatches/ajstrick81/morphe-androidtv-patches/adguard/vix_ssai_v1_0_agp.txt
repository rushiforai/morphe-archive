! Target: Android TV build 4.46.0_tv  |  Status: CONFIRMED via PC HAR capture
! Modeled on the Fox One SSAI v5.0 ruleset.
!
! v1.0 CHANGELOG (from v0.1 draft):
!   - Captured a real AdGuard log (PC client, web player, same backend ViX
!     uses on Android TV) during a live channel session with a scheduled
!     mid-roll. This confirmed the actual ad-insertion mechanism and made
!     §2/3/4 of the v0.1 draft (segment-path intercept, manifest $replace,
!     param stripping) UNNECESSARY. See "WHAT THE HAR CONFIRMED" below.
! ============================================================
!
! WHAT THE HAR CONFIRMED:
!   - ViX's live ad system is GOOGLE DAI POD SERVING, fronted by Lura as a
!     reseller/relay (NOT classic client-built VAST/VMAP, NOT Yospace).
!     The actual ad session request observed:
!       POST dai.google.com/ssai/pods/api/v1/network/<N>/custom_asset/<asset>/stream
!            ?...&vad_type=linear&ad_rule=1&vpos=midroll&mridx=0&pod=1
!            &iu=/<N>/vix.tv/viewer/live/web_desktop/<channel>
!            &cust_params=stream_type%3Dlive%26...
!     This is the DAI "stream request" call — it returns a GAM session
!     (a stream id) that Lura then stitches into the manifest.
!   - The player then fetches the actual playable manifest from LURA, not
!     Google, at:
!       dcs4-live.mp.lura.live/server/podserve2/<id>/manifest.mpd?gam_stream_id=<id>...   (live)
!       dcs4-vod.mp.lura.live/vod/p/manifest.mpd?encp=...                                  (VOD)
!     "podserve2" / "/vod/p/" = Lura's wrapper around Google DAI Pod
!     Serving — this is where ad pods get stitched into the manifest
!     server-side (true SSAI: indistinguishable from content at the HTTP
!     layer once stitched).
!   - CRITICAL FINDING: in the capture, dai.google.com and
!     pubads.g.doubleclick.net were ALREADY blocked by AdGuard's stock
!     filters (FiredFilterId 15 / -10074 — not a custom rule). The DAI
!     stream-request POST never got a response (StatusCode 0, connection
!     refused). Immediately after, the manifest request to
!     dcs4-live.mp.lura.live/server/podserve2/.../manifest.mpd used
!     gam_stream_id=mock-stream — i.e. when the GAM/DAI session can't be
!     established, Lura's client SDK falls back to a LOCAL MOCK STREAM ID
!     and requests a plain (non-ad) stitched manifest. Playback continued
!     normally afterward (live segments kept streaming from
!     vix-gmedia-01.mp.lura.live with no errors).
!   - Segment URLs for both live and VOD give NO path-level signal that
!     would distinguish an ad segment from a content segment:
!       live:  vix-gmedia-01.mp.lura.live/live/ephemeral/<token>/<channel>/<bitrate>/<id>/<seq>/segment_<n>-video.mp4
!       vod:   vix-vodgmedia-03.mp.lura.live/pkg/v2/<blob>/video-<hash>-<n>.mp4
!     Sequence numbers are continuous and opaque — this CONFIRMS true SSAI
!     stitching (ad and content segments are the same shape, same host,
!     same naming scheme). §2 of the v0.1 draft (ad-segment path intercept)
!     is therefore not just unconfirmed, it's IMPOSSIBLE: there is no
!     selector that separates an ad segment from a content segment once
!     stitching has occurred. The only viable intervention point is
!     upstream of stitching — i.e. the DAI stream-request call itself.
!   - Because of the above, §3 (manifest $replace for EXT-X-DATERANGE/SCTE)
!     and §4 (GAM param stripping on the Lura play/manifest endpoints) are
!     also dropped: there's nothing left to strip once the DAI session
!     never starts — the manifest Lura serves back is already ad-free
!     (mock-stream), not an ad-laden manifest needing surgical edits.
!   - Cert pinning: still only PerimeterX in-code (confirmed in earlier dex
!     analysis); the hosts above are not pinned, so the companion
!     network_security_config patch (already in the morphe repo) is
!     sufficient to let AdGuard see this traffic on Android TV.
!
! NET EFFECT: this ruleset is now a precise host/endpoint blocklist around
! the DAI session-establishment call, rather than a content-layer rewrite
! list. Much simpler and lower-risk than the Fox One ruleset (which has to
! fight Yospace's pre-built ad-laden manifests at the content layer).
! ============================================================

! ------------------------------------------------------------
! 1. GOOGLE DAI POD SERVING — block the session BEFORE it forms
! This is the entire fix. Confirmed by HAR: blocking these two endpoints
! causes Lura to fall back to gam_stream_id=mock-stream (a content-only,
! non-ad manifest) instead of stalling or erroring. Hard block (not
! redirect) is safe here because the call happens before any manifest
! request, not mid-segment — there is no stream to interrupt yet.
! ------------------------------------------------------------
||dai.google.com/ssai/pods/api/v1/network/*/custom_asset/*/stream$important
||dai.google.com^$important
||pubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ads$important
||securepubads.g.doubleclick.net/gampad/ads$important
||imasdk.googleapis.com^$important

! 8. DO NOT BLOCK (would break app/login/playback)
!   - PerimeterX (collector-*.pxchk.net / px-cdn.net / px-cloud.net) — bot
!     defense, CERT-PINNED in-code. Confirmed present in HAR
!     (collector-pxk9q1jq56.{pxchk.net,px-cdn.net,px-cloud.net}). Blocking
!     or MITM-ing it can lock you out of login/playback.
!   - access*.mp.lura.live / license.mp.lura.live / drm3.mp.lura.live —
!     DRM/license/fairplay. Confirmed present in HAR (drm3.mp.lura.live/fp).
!   - dcs4-live / dcs4-vod / mp.lura.live media+manifest hosts — must stay
!     reachable; §1 makes them ad-free without needing to touch them.
!   - newrelic (js-agent.newrelic.com), appsflyer (websdk.appsflyer.com) —
!     marketing/crash telemetry, not ad-insertion. Optional to block, but
!     don't redirect/rewrite — host-block only if you want them gone.
